Wireless Power Transfer System Deisgn & Energy Harvesting Power Management Circuit

Designing Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) coils for implantable medical devices requires balancing stringent physical constraints with optimal electrical performance. Our laboratory has developed an advanced iterative co-optimization framework that systematically bridges geometrical parameter sweeping with system-level electrical performance evaluations.

The core of our methodology is an automated loop driven by Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). The process begins with user-defined specifications, including maximum Rx outer diameter, resonance frequency, coil distance, and tissue properties. We perform comprehensive geometric sweeps across both Tx and Rx coils—varying parameters such as outer diameter (Dₒᵤₜ), trace width (w), spacing (s), and number of turns (N). Traditional analytical models often fall short in complex biological environments. To overcome this, our PINN-enhanced models—pre-trained on extensive simulation and experimental data—refine the calculation of fundamental electrical parameters (L, R, C, k) and accurately predict tissue-induced parasitic effects (Cₜᵢₛₛᵤₑ and Rₜᵢₛₛᵤₑ).

Through this iterative loop, the refined parameters are continuously fed into system-level performance calculators. Depending on the specific clinical requirement, our algorithm actively assesses and diverges into specialized optimization paths, ultimately generating customized coil designs optimized for either maximum Power Transfer Efficiency (PTE) or maximum Power Delivered to the Load (PDL).

The clinical translation of electromedicine is frequently hindered by the surgical burden of replacing depleted batteries and the inherently large volume of conventional implantable devices. Our research addresses this challenge by proposing a hybrid charging architecture that reduces the required battery volume by up to 80% while maintaining robust operational capability.

To compensate for the reduced battery capacity, we integrate continuous internal Energy Harvesting (EH) with fast-charging WPT. While in vivo EH technologies like Triboelectric Nanogenerators (TENG) and Magnetoelastic Generators (MEG) provide a continuous power source, they are limited by low output power (requiring hours to days for charging). We resolve this limitation by seamlessly incorporating our optimized WPT system, which is capable of delivering high power for rapid charging within minutes.

The core of this application is our custom-designed Power Management Integrated Circuit (PMIC). The system architecture features a smart selection circuit that efficiently manages multiple rectified inputs (TEEH, MEEH, and WPT), routing stable power to the central controller and battery. To validate clinical feasibility, our fully fabricated charge systems—including the Tx/Rx coils and PMIC—are rigorously evaluated through in vivo charging tests utilizing murine models. These tests confirm not only the rapid voltage charging curves of the supercapacitors and batteries but also ensure strict adherence to thermal safety limits during active power transfer.
